With its growing internationally orientated population, businesses and organizations Utrecht is a pro-active player in the international community. Utrecht has been declared Europeans Most Competitive Region (pdf, 2 Mb) by the European Commission and is leading many international rankings nowadays such as the top ten World’s best cycling cities (CNN 2015) or the Guardians 'Where to go' 2015 ranking. 

International Economic Agenda

With the International Economic Agenda the municipality is aiming to create an international attractive climate for our citizens, businesses, entrepreneurs, expats, students and visitors. 

Some examples:

  • The Expat Center in the New City Hall and the International School Utrecht are important facilities to attract international talent.
  • The economic branding of our region attracts businesses, entrepreneurs, visitors and investors. The Utrecht Toolkit provides English information about for all stakeholders interested in Utrecht.
  • FilmCommission Utrecht promotes Utrecht as a smart location for filming, providing service and advice, a wide range of locations and atmospheres, physical space and an unparalleled range of suppliers. Filming in Utrecht supports local economy and the strengthening of Utrecht Film City and its talents. 
  • Utrecht is a Multilingual Hotspot and offers a high educated workforce

European Strategy

We defined a European strategy (pdf, 278 kB) in which we focus on our ambitions to promote the city of Utrecht as an interesting city to live, work, study, invest or visit. We attract European funding to invest in urban healthy living and facilitate exchange of knowledge with European cities.

Some examples:

  • As an active member of the EUROCITIES network, Utrecht is working with 130 cities and institutions of the European Union to ensure that European policies and legislation meet the needs of our citizens. As chair of the EUROCITIES Environment Forum in 2015 Utrecht focuses on shaping European policies on air quality, renewable energy, energy efficiency, noise, waste, green areas and urban biodiversity.
  • Through focused networking and cooperation Utrecht profiles itself as an interesting  and innovative European city and a potential project partner.  In the framework of its dynamic  Connected Cities Network Utrecht wants to connect itself with a selection of European cities, focusing on its current themes and ambitions.  
  • Utrecht and the other large Dutch cities (Amsterdam, Rotterdam and The Hague) have joined forces in the G4-alliance in Brussels.
  • Utrecht participates in several EU cooperation projects. Utrecht is partner in the ‘Kansen voor West’ Operational Program (ERDF, 2014-2020) which focuses on innovation and low carbon economy.  As an Intermediate Body, Utrecht also implements an Integrated Territorial Investment strategy (ITI) that is funded by ERDF/ESF.

Utrecht Connected Worldwide

Utrecht is transforming from a Dutch city to a global city, spreading out her wings all over the world. Nowadays the organizations in Utrecht work with their counterparts outside Europe. They are discovering their possibilities in Asia and the US. Because of these developments, the city authority facilitates the organizations by opening doors and building bridges between Utrecht and cities outside Europe. From creative entrepreneurs to international festivals: if it’s valuable to the international position of Utrecht, then there is a reason to connect Utrecht to the rest of the World.

Some examples:

  • The China Light Festival attracted 90.000 visitors and strengthened the relations with the Chinese community in Utrecht. The amount of Chinese visitors and students in Utrecht is growing.
  • Creative entrepreneurs are getting the change to promote and sell their products in the city of Portland, Oregon (USA). This way, small entrepreneurs are given the opportunity to do business outside Utrecht.
  • Utrecht was the first Dutch city where the Indian society organized the Gandhi Walk, in remembrance of Mahatma Gandhi. This led to the preparations of a statue of Gandhi in one of the hotspots of the city. This way, the Indian society feels more at home in Utrecht and the city becomes attractive for the thousands of Indian expats and visitors.

Human Rights City

Their international knowledge, experience and contacts greatly enrich society in Utrecht. The city authority supports activities and exchanges that help foster respect for human dignity. Working in partnership with various organizations, the city is striving to strengthen human rights at the local level.

Sustainable Development Goals

Many organizations and individuals in Utrecht support the United Nations' Millennium Development Campaign. The 8 goals of this Campaign formed the basis for sustainable development and reducing poverty worldwide. As a 'Millennium City', Utrecht supported the many international projects undertaken by local organizations. In 2015 the United Nations will define the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG). The city of Utrecht is working with local organizations such as Utrecht Fairtrade and Team2105Utrecht our focus for the SDG for the coming years
